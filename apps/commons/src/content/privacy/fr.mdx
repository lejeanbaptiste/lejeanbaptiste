## Data Collection and Storage

### What data is collected

LEAF-Writer Commons collects data for three main purposes:

- **Necessary information** _(required)_ for the proper functioning of the website. We collect and store your browser's language, cookie consent, and preferences.
- **Error monitoring** _(required)_ to maintain site reliability and improve user experience. We use a third-party error monitoring service to detect and diagnose technical issues. This service captures information about errors and application behaviour only. No personal data is transmitted to or stored by this service or by LEAF-Writer Commons.
- **Authentication** _(optional)_ to allow users to sign annotations and use their GitHub or GitLab account to load and save files. The authentication process uses the Linked Infrastructure for Networked Cultural Scholarship’s ([LINCS Project](https://lincsproject.ca/)) authentication service to sign you in and retain minimal data about you. We collect your email and username from your preferred linked identity providers (e.g., GitHub, GitLab, and ORCID). By signing in, you allow the LINCS Project and LEAF-Writer Commons to collect and store this services access tokens to allow loading and saving files on GitHub or GitLab from LEAF-Writer Commons, as well as the minimum personal information you publicly share on these identity providers, such as your name, avatar, and public repositories. Authentication is optional. You do not need to sign in to use LEAF-Writer Commons.
- **Analytics** _(optional)_ to measure the site's performance. We use Google Analytics to track user behaviour on the website. Google Analytics can track your location using your computer's IP address and device information. Analytics is optional and can be turned off at any time. We do not allow Google Analytics to collect data for personalization or advertising.

### How Data is Stored

LEAF-Writer Commons stores your data on your browser using a combination of cookies, local storage, and IndexedDB. You can inspect them at any time using your browser's developer tools.

LINCS Project stores your data in a secure digital infrastructure provided by The Digital Research Alliance of Canada (DRAC), located in Canada.

### How Long Data is Stored For

Personal data we process for any purpose(s) shall not be kept longer than is necessary for the purpose(s) for which it is collected. The duration varies by purpose and location.

- Information stored as cookies can be kept for up to 1 year. After that, they are either renewed or removed from your browser.
- Information stored in your local storage and IndexedDB is kept indefinitely until you log out of LEAF-Writer Commons.
- Information stored in your LINCS Project is stored indefinitely until you revoke access or delete your account.
